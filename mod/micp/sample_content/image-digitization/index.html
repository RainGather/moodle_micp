Step 4

Quality vs file size recommendation

Locked

Summarize the tradeoff

Use the current preview as your evidence. The goal is not “max quality at any cost,” but a choice that fits the use case.

Low resolutionVery small file, but edges and small details dissolve quickly.
Low bit depthSmall file, but gradients band and shadows collapse together.
Balanced settingsKeep enough structure and tone for the task while controlling size.
  • Thumbnail or preview images can often accept lower resolution than print graphics.
  • Bit depth matters most when you need smooth shadows, fog, or photographic shading.
  • The “best” file is the one that meets its purpose with the fewest unnecessary bits.
